The coronavirus Covid-19 is affecting all around the world including Myanmar. Covid-19 is causing an immense potential impact on climate change, especially in air pollution. According to the above two charts, air pollution indication is inversely proportional to number of Covid-19 positive cases. In first wave ( From February to April), air quality index is a little changed into 80. During the middle of 2020, low air pollution emissions are found because of commitment projects such as closing schools, universities and restaurants, Covid-19 lockdown, quarantine and stay-home campaign and so on. In end of August, the numbers of confirmed positive cases per day increased rapidly. It led to a drop in air quality index from 40 to 20. Now, the AQI is significantly decreased compared to AQI before Covid-19.
